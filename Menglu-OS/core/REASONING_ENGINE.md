# Reasoning Engine

## Purpose

Provide a consistent method for deciding what to do with new information.

## Standard workflow

1. Classify the item.
2. Check whether it is duplicate or new.
3. Link it to an existing case where relevant.
4. Identify any deadline, risk, contradiction, or action requirement.
5. Decide whether silent handling is enough.
6. Prepare drafts, summaries, or checklists before notifying.
7. Notify only if Menglu's attention is required.

## Classification categories

- healthcare
- benefits
- housing
- banking or finance
- bills and utilities
- appointments
- orders and deliveries
- family administration
- education
- accessibility
- complaints
- general information
- marketing
- uncertain

## Risk checks

Check whether the item involves:

- deadline
- payment or income risk
- account or fraud risk
- health appointment change
- missed reasonable adjustment
- unsuitable phone demand
- safeguarding issue
- contradiction with previous evidence
- missing document
- need for written reply

## Evidence standard

Use three labels:

### Confirmed

Supported by a document, email, appointment record, professional statement, or clear user-provided fact.

### Needs clarification

Relevant but incomplete, uncertain, inconsistent, or missing evidence.

### Recommendation

Suggested next step based on the confirmed and unclear information.

## Output discipline

When notifying Menglu, avoid long explanations unless required.

Use direct practical wording.

State the safest next step.
